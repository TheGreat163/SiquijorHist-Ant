Siquijor Heritage and Antiques

Siquijor is not only known for its beautiful beaches and lush nature, but also for its rich history and culture. The island is home to various historical sites, old churches, and unique antiques that tell the story of its colonial past and native traditions. The mystical and magical aura of the island is reflected in the age-old artifacts and architectural wonders that continue to attract curious explorers.

Historical Sites

Siquijor boasts some fascinating historical landmarks like the century-old St. Francis of Assisi Church in the town of Lazi and the old Enchanted Balete Tree, which has become a cultural symbol of the island's mystique.

Antique Collections

The island’s antique shops and museums house a wide range of preserved items from the Spanish era, including furniture, religious icons, and other relics that highlight Siquijor’s colonial heritage.

Guides to Travel in Siquijor

✈️ By Airplane

Fly into Dumaguete Airport, then take a ferry to Siquijor. Direct flights are available from Manila and Cebu.

⛴️ Travel by Boat

Ferries and fast crafts run daily between Siquijor and nearby islands like Dumaguete, Bohol, and Cebu. Major ports include Larena and Siquijor Town, offering easy sea access.

🚐 By Land

On the island, get around via jeepney, tricycle, or rent a scooter to explore Siquijor at your own pace.
