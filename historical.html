Lazi Church & Convent

Location: Siquijor, Philippines

Built: 1884

Style: Baroque

Significance: Declared as a National Cultural Treasure.

Brief History

Lazi Church, officially known as the San Isidro Labrador Parish Church, was constructed in 1884 by Spanish missionaries. Located in the tranquil town of Lazi in Siquijor, it features Baroque architectural elements and is made of coral stones and hardwood. The adjacent convent, one of the largest in the country, was built in 1891 and served as a residence for clergy and a center for religious education. The church and convent complex has been declared a National Cultural Treasure by the National Museum of the Philippines.

Our Lady of Divine Providence Church

Location: Maria, Siquijor, Philippines

Built: Late 1800s

Style: Colonial Fortress-style

Significance: Known for its dark-skinned statue of the Virgin Mary, locally revered as “Black Maria.”

Brief History

The Our Lady of Divine Providence Church, located in the town of Maria in Siquijor, was built during the Spanish colonial era. It features a strong, fortress-like design typical of churches built to withstand both natural and human threats. Inside the church is a mysterious and iconic statue of the Virgin Mary, known as “Black Maria,” which is deeply woven into the town’s religious traditions and local legends. The church continues to serve as a spiritual center for the community and remains a site of historical and cultural interest.

St. Augustine of Hippo Church

Location: San Juan, Siquijor, Philippines

Built: Early 1900s

Style: Colonial-inspired

Significance: Dedicated to St. Augustine of Hippo and serves as a central place of worship for the town of San Juan.

Brief History

St. Augustine of Hippo Church, located in the municipality of San Juan, Siquijor, is a modest yet spiritually significant Roman Catholic church. Named after the famed theologian and philosopher, the church has served as a cornerstone of the local Catholic community for over a century. Though simpler in architecture compared to older Spanish-era churches on the island, it stands out for its tranquil surroundings and deep-rooted traditions. It plays an active role in local festivities and religious observances, reflecting the enduring faith of its parishioners.

Our Lady of Mount Carmel Church

Location: Enrique Villanueva, Siquijor, Philippines

Built: 20th Century

Style: Modern with Traditional Elements

Significance: Serves as the spiritual center of the town and a symbol of local devotion.

Brief History

Our Lady of Mount Carmel Church is located in the peaceful municipality of Enrique Villanueva in Siquijor. Though more recent in construction compared to other historic churches on the island, it reflects the town’s strong religious identity and cultural values. The church is dedicated to Our Lady of Mount Carmel, the patroness of the town, and plays a central role in annual festivities and community life. It remains an important place for worship and gathering for the local Catholic faithful.

Lazi Convent

Location: Siquijor, Philippines

Built: 1891

Style: Colonial Baroque

Significance: One of the largest and oldest convents in the Philippines.

Brief History

The Lazi Convent, located beside the San Isidro Labrador Parish Church in Lazi, Siquijor, was constructed in 1891 by Filipino artisans under Spanish supervision. Built primarily of coral stones and hardwood, it served as a residence for the clergy and as a center for missionary work and religious education. Its size and architectural integrity make it one of the most significant ecclesiastical structures from the Spanish colonial period in the Philippines. The convent now houses a museum showcasing religious artifacts and local history, and has been declared a National Cultural Treasure by the National Museum of the Philippines.

St. Francis of Assisi Church

Location: Siquijor Town, Siquijor, Philippines

Built: 1795

Style: Colonial / Spanish-era architecture

Significance: One of the oldest churches in Siquijor, located near the port

Brief History

The St. Francis of Assisi Church is the first religious structure you’ll encounter when arriving at the port of Siquijor. Built in 1795 during the Spanish colonial period, this stone church stands as a testament to the island’s deep Catholic roots. It features thick coral stone walls and an adjoining bell tower, which historically served as a lookout against sea invaders. The church remains active today and is an important spiritual and cultural landmark in the province.

Old Enchanted Balete Tree

Location: Campalanas, Lazi, Siquijor, Philippines

Estimated Age: Over 400 years old

Type: Balete (Banyan) Tree

Significance: Famous natural landmark tied to folklore and local healing traditions

Brief History

The Old Enchanted Balete Tree in Campalanas, Lazi is one of the most iconic natural attractions in Siquijor. Estimated to be more than 400 years old, this massive balete (or banyan) tree is shrouded in mystery and local legend. Many believe the tree is enchanted, associated with stories of elemental spirits and ancient healing practices. At its base, a natural spring flows into a man-made pool where visitors can enjoy a unique fish spa experience. The tree has become a cultural symbol of Siquijor's mystical heritage and is a popular stop for both tourists and spiritual seekers.

St. Vincent Ferrer Church

Location: Larena, Siquijor, Philippines

Built: Early 20th Century

Denomination: Roman Catholic

Significance: A prominent church named after St. Vincent Ferrer, known for its heritage value and local religious importance

Brief History

St. Vincent Ferrer Church is located in the municipality of Larena, Siquijor. Dedicated to St. Vincent Ferrer, the patron saint known for his missionary work and miracles, this church serves as the spiritual center of the local Catholic community. The structure dates back to the early 20th century and features modest yet enduring architecture reflective of Spanish ecclesiastical influences. Over the years, the church has undergone restorations but remains a peaceful and culturally significant landmark, hosting regular Masses and local festivals in honor of its patron saint.

Watch Tower - Siquijor

Location: Near St. Francis of Assisi Church, Siquijor Town

Built: Late 1800s (Spanish Colonial Period)

Type: Coastal Defense Structure

Significance: One of the oldest Spanish-era watchtowers in the Visayas, built to guard against Moro raids

Brief History

The Watch Tower of Siquijor, located near the St. Francis of Assisi Church, is a remnant of the island's colonial defense system during the Spanish era. Built in the late 1800s, the tower was strategically positioned to spot incoming Moro pirate raids that frequently threatened the Visayan islands. Constructed from coral stones and lime mortar, it reflects the Spanish colonial architectural style adapted for coastal fortifications. Today, the structure stands as a historical landmark and silent witness to Siquijor’s turbulent yet resilient past.

Cang-Isok House

Location: Enrique Villanueva, Siquijor, Philippines

Built: Estimated in the mid-1800s

Type: Traditional Filipino stilt house (Bahay Kubo)

Significance: Considered the oldest surviving house in Siquijor

Brief History

The Cang-Isok House, located in the quiet town of Enrique Villanueva in Siquijor, is believed to be the oldest existing house on the island. Built in the mid-1800s, it is a traditional nipa hut made of hardwood and nipa palm, elevated on stilts to withstand flooding and heat. Despite its age, the house remains a cultural treasure, representing the traditional way of life during the Spanish colonial period. While no longer occupied, it stands as a heritage structure and reminder of Siquijor’s simple and resilient past.

Capilay Spring Park

Location: San Juan, Siquijor, Philippines

Type: Natural Spring and Public Park

Features: Spring-fed pool, picnic area, shaded trees

Significance: Popular local spot for relaxation and social gatherings

About Capilay Spring Park

Capilay Spring Park is a natural spring-turned-public park located in the heart of San Juan, Siquijor. The spring's cool, fresh water flows year-round, feeding into a series of open pools where both locals and tourists can enjoy a refreshing swim. Surrounded by trees, the park offers plenty of shade and open space for picnics and gatherings. Admission is free, making it a popular place to unwind, especially on weekends. The water originates from a natural underground source and is considered very clean and clear.
