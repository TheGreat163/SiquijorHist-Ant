Common Tarditional Tools

Wooden Mortar and Pestle

Carved from local hardwood, this set was essential for grinding grains and herbs. Used in both kitchens and by traditional healers in Siquijor.

Traditional Tool Grinder

A manually operated grinder used for sharpening farm tools. Commonly seen near homes and vital for maintaining daily tools in rural Siquijor.

Traditional Hand Mill

Composed of two stones, this mill was used to grind rice and corn. Operated by hand, it reflects the self-sufficient spirit of early islanders.

Traditional Coconut Grater

Made of wood with a metal blade, it’s used to grate coconut meat, essential in Siquijorian cuisine and oil preparation.

Traditional Wooden Plow

Made of strong hardwood and pulled by carabao, this plow was crucial for tilling rice and corn fields in pre-mechanized farming.

Traditional Herbal and Medicinal

Used by healers for storing and preparing herbs, these tools are linked to Siquijor’s traditional healing practices and spiritual rituals.

Siquijor Church Bell

Cast in bronze and engraved with religious symbols, this bell signaled mass and events—echoing faith and unity across the island.

Wooden Mortar and Pestle

Location: Siquijor, Philippines

Built: Traditional use dating back centuries

Style: Indigenous Filipino Craft

Significance: Essential tool in traditional Filipino cooking and herbal medicine preparation, symbolizing cultural heritage.

Materials

Hand-carved from durable hardwood, sourced locally from the forests of Siquijor.

Design

Simple yet functional, consisting of a large bowl (mortar) and a heavy club-shaped tool (pestle) designed for manual grinding.

Appearance

Rustic and smooth with natural wood grain patterns; typically shows signs of wear from extensive use.

Function

Used to grind, crush, and pulverize spices, grains, and herbs for cooking and medicinal purposes.

Characteristics

Represents traditional craftsmanship and is a symbol of community, self-sufficiency, and connection to nature.

Traditional Wooden Plow

Location: Siquijor, Philippines

Built: Traditional, used for centuries

Style: Indigenous Filipino Agricultural Tool

Significance: Essential farming implement symbolizing local agricultural heritage and sustainable practices.

Materials

Hand-carved hardwood such as narra or molave, often reinforced with metal tips.

Design

Long wooden shaft with a curved blade designed to be pulled by carabaos for tilling soil.

Sturdy, weathered, with a rustic look stained by years of contact with soil and water.

Function

Characteristics

Traditional Herbal and Medicinal Tools

Location:Siquijor, Philippines

Built: Used for centuries, with origins tracing back to pre-colonial times

Style: Indigenous and handcrafted, utilizing natural materials

Significance: Represents the rich folk healing traditions of Siquijor, essential in community health and spiritual practices

Materials

Made from bamboo, coconut shells, hardwood, and native fibers, combined with local medicinal herbs.

Design

Handcrafted with simple, practical forms suited for grinding, mixing, and storing herbal preparations.

Appearance

Earthy and natural, often showing signs of wear from frequent use in traditional healing rituals.

Function

Used to prepare herbal infusions, poultices, and remedies for physical and spiritual ailments.

Characteristics

Represents the rich folk healing tradition of Siquijor, central to “bolo-bolo” and other native healing practices.

Old Siquijor Church Bell

Location: St. Francis of Assisi Church, Siquijor Town

Estimated Age: Over 200 years old

Type: Spanish-era Bronze Church Bell

Significance: Symbol of faith and colonial heritage, still used in religious ceremonies.

Materials

Made of cast bronze, with traditional Spanish inscriptions and religious symbols.

Design

Classic colonial-era bell with flared base and looped crown for hanging; often decorated with Christian emblems.

Appearance

Weathered surface with patina from decades of use; mounted in a stone belfry near the church entrance.

Function

Used to signal Mass, religious events, emergencies, and mark time for the community.

Characteristics

Deep resonant tone, iconic historical value, and considered one of the oldest bells still functioning in the Visayas.
